Red lentil, chickpea & chilli soup

Description

Being 100% honest, I don't really know much about this recipe but I needed recipes and nothing was coming to mind so I found this and was like ah yes let's use it. It seems pretty healthy as well so that's a win, probably full of antioxidants and related healthy things. So yeah,,, enjoy.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • Large pinch of chilli flakes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 chopped red onion
  • 140g red split lentils
  • 850ml vegetable stock
  • 400g canned tomatoes (whole or chopped)
  • 200g can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • Small bunch coriander, roughly chopped
  • 4 tbsp 0% fat Greek yoghurt

Preparation

  1. Heat large saucepan and dry-fry 2tsp cumin seeds and a large pinch of chilli flakes for ~1min, or until they start to jump around the pan and release aromas.
  2. Add 1 tbsp olive oil and 1 chopped red onion. Cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in 140g red split lentils, 850ml vegetable stock and a 400g can of tomatoes and bring to the boil. Simmer for 15 mins until the lentils have softened.
  4. Place the soup in a food processor or blend with stick blender until it is a rough puree.
  5. Pour the soup back into the pan and add a 200g can of drained and rinsed chickpeas.
  6. Heat gently, season well and stir in a small bunch of chopped coriander, reserving a few leaves to serve.
  7. Finish serving with 4tbsp of 0% fat Greek yoghurt and extra coriander leaves.
